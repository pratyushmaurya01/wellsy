# Behavior Profile

## Personality Overview

I am a highly introverted person who prefers meaningful interactions over frequent socialization. I rarely start conversations without a clear reason and generally keep to myself.

Although I naturally prefer solitude, I am actively trying to improve my communication skills and become more comfortable interacting with people.

I value simplicity, honesty, and authenticity more than status or appearance.

---

# Decision Making Style

My decisions are often influenced by emotions rather than pure logic.

I tend to trust people quickly and often try to help others even when it comes at a personal cost. This is one of my biggest weaknesses because I sometimes prioritize other people more than myself.

I usually choose kindness and cooperation first, even when it may not be the most practical decision.

---

# Learning Style

I learn best through:

* Building projects
* Solving problems
* Repeated practice
* Learning from mistakes

Whenever I make a mistake, I try not to repeat it again.

I strongly believe that failure is useful only when lessons are extracted from it.

I prefer practical learning over theoretical learning.

---

# Problem Solving Approach

When facing a difficult problem, I rarely quit immediately.

My typical process is:

1. Try solving independently.
2. Search on Google.
3. Use ChatGPT.
4. Watch YouTube explanations.
5. Take a short break.
6. Return and try again.

I only give up when I become convinced that I currently lack the knowledge required to solve the problem.

Persistence is one of my strongest qualities.

---

# Work Style

I enjoy working both independently and in teams.

However, I prefer teams where:

* Members contribute equally.
* People are humble.
* Collaboration is genuine.
* There is mutual respect.

I dislike environments where people constantly try to prove superiority rather than solve problems.

---

# Planning vs Execution

One of my recurring challenges is over-planning.

I often spend significant time thinking, researching, and preparing before taking action.

As a result:

* Planning is stronger than execution.
* Ideas are often ahead of implementation.
* Consistency becomes difficult.

Improving execution speed is a major personal goal.

---

# Stress Management

When stressed, I generally try to stay occupied with work rather than completely disengaging.

Activities that help reduce stress:

* Listening to music
* Singing
* Coding
* Learning something new
* Solving problems

Music plays an important role in emotional regulation.

---

# Motivation System

I am primarily motivated by self-improvement rather than competition.

Comparing myself with highly successful people often decreases motivation.

Instead, I prefer measuring progress against my previous self.

Core belief:

"Being slightly better than yesterday is enough progress."

---

Technical Strengths:

Personal Strengths:

* Adaptability

# Weaknesses

Major weaknesses include:

* Inconsistency
* Procrastination
* Daydreaming
* Low self-confidence
* Over-trusting people
* Emotional decision making
* Communication challenges

These weaknesses often impact execution more than technical ability.

---

# Fears

Primary concerns include:

* Financial instability
* Remaining average despite effort
* Failing to reach potential

These fears often act as both motivation and pressure.

---

# Communication Style

I prefer direct communication.

I appreciate honest criticism more than artificial praise.

If I make a mistake, I prefer someone to point it out directly.

Similarly, I tend to speak honestly and directly, sometimes too quickly before fully considering emotions.

If I realize I am wrong, I usually apologize without hesitation.

---

# Interests

Activities I naturally enjoy:

* Coding
* Solving DSA problems
* Learning technology
* Exploring new ideas
* Listening to music
* Building projects

A free day is usually spent learning, coding, experimenting, or exploring new technical concepts.

---

# Project Selection Philosophy

When choosing projects, I look for:

* Unique ideas
* Interesting problems
* Practical usefulness
* Learning opportunities

I actively avoid building projects simply because they are common tutorials.

I prefer exploring less crowded ideas and combining concepts from different domains.

---

# Personal Philosophy

I believe growth happens gradually through consistent effort.

Success is not achieved in a single moment but through small improvements repeated over long periods.

My goal is to keep moving forward every day, even if progress is slow.

I identify strongly with the idea of continuously moving toward a goal rather than seeking immediate results.
